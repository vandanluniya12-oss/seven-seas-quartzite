There is one quarry. One deposit. One address in the Aravalli highlands. When this vein is exhausted, this stone is finished. There is no second source.

Seven Seas Quartzite was formed over hundreds of millions of years as sandstone in the Aravalli mountain range was buried under the earth's crust and subjected to heat and pressure so extreme that the quartz grains fused entirely. What emerged was a metamorphic stone harder, denser, and more visually compelling than almost anything in the natural world.

PASHAN was founded by Rajeev Lodha, who brings over twenty years of quarrying expertise to every decision made at the source. Rajeev established the Blue Dunes Granite quarry and was the first person in India to identify and bring to market Alaska White, Copenhagen, and Patagonia quartzite — three stones now widely known across the global stone industry. Three years ago he started PASHAN, anchored by the Seven Seas deposit. Vandan Luniya, who relocated from Bangkok and Boston to Ajmer to be closer to the work, is a partner in the business and the primary point of contact for all project and supply enquiries.

For the first three years of production, every block extracted went exclusively to Italy. Italian fabricators, who work with the finest material on earth, selected Seven Seas above everything available to them. That exclusive relationship was a validation no marketing claim can replicate. In 2025, PASHAN began selectively opening Seven Seas to project partners outside Italy. Every allocation is a decision.

The colour

Blue, grey, and green are present simultaneously in every slab and shift in dominance depending on light source, angle, and time of day. Morning light pulls the blue forward. Afternoon reveals the grey. Under warm incandescence at evening, the green comes through and the room changes. It is not a trend. It is geology. A designer who understands the light conditions of their project and uses that knowledge during slab selection will always produce the strongest result.
